Project Site Plan

Official Soccer Club Website

Site Name

Proposed Domain: CulersFC.com

Reason for selection: The name utilizes "Culers", which is the historic, globally recognized nickname for FC Barcelona supporters. It instantly creates an authentic identity, establishes branding alignment, and is highly memorable for target fans and prospective local talents looking to identify with the club's philosophy.

Site Purpose

The purpose of this 4-page website is to serve as the central informational hub for the soccer club. It bridges the gap between the club's administration, existing fan base, and prospective players by fulfilling three main scopes:

  • Brand Identity: Showcasing historical achievements, foundational values, and direct club timelines.
  • News & Operations: Distributing live tracking updates regarding roster shifts, transfer market activities, and staff rosters.
  • Talent Acquisition: Providing an interactive mechanism via an application form to allow underground talents to formally apply for club trials.

Target Audience Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Aspiring Athlete

"I am a competitive local striker looking for an academy or official team tryout. Where can I submit my athletic stats and video reels to get scouted by this club?"

Site Answer: This user will navigate directly to the 4th page, which contains the specialized sign-up/trial registration form built explicitly to capture applicant profiles.

Scenario 2: The Dedicated Supporter

"I want to check if the recent rumors about our new midfield signing are official, and see who is currently managing our tactical coaching staff."

Site Answer: This user will visit the 2nd page to read verified transfer market news updates, and the 3rd page to view images and profiles of the existing coaching staff and executive boards.

Color Scheme

The visual identity relies strictly on the official palette of FC Barcelona to evoke passion, tradition, and authority:

  • Deep Blue (#004d98): Applied as the primary layout structural anchor. Used for header backgrounds, primary page section containers, and main level-1 headers.
  • Burgundy/Maroon (#a50044): Applied as the primary structural pairing asset. Used for secondary accents, secondary header typography, navigation anchors, and element borders.
  • Accent Gold (#edbb00): Used strictly for critical call-outs, interactive states, validation lines, and structural dividers to ensure high readability.

Typography

To establish an athletic, clean, and highly readable layout, two distinct web fonts are imported from Google Fonts:

  • Font 1: Montserrat (Bold 700) - Applied to all major headings (H1, H2, H3). Its wide, geometric structure offers a commanding, athletic presence suitable for a sports brand.
  • Font 2: Open Sans (Regular 400 / Semi-Bold 600) - Applied to all structural paragraphs, form labels, list elements, and data tables. It offers maximum cross-device legibility.
